# Code of Conduct

This project is open to public collaboration, but not to abuse.

## Expected behavior

Participants are expected to:

- communicate directly and respectfully
- critique ideas, code, and product decisions without personal attacks
- assume good faith until there is evidence otherwise
- keep technical disagreement concrete and specific
- respect maintainers' time and review boundaries

## Unacceptable behavior

The following are not acceptable:

- harassment, threats, or intimidation
- hate speech or identity-based attacks
- repeated bad-faith argumentation or derailing
- doxxing, stalking, or invasive behavior
- spam, trolling, or intentionally disruptive conduct

## Maintainer authority

Project maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or remove content, and may restrict participation, when behavior makes collaboration worse or unsafe.

## Reporting

If someone is behaving in a way that makes the project unsafe or unworkable, report it privately to the maintainer instead of escalating publicly.

## Enforcement

Possible responses include:

- a warning
- removal of comments or contributions
- temporary or permanent participation restrictions

The standard is simple: if your behavior makes it harder for others to collaborate, expect intervention.
